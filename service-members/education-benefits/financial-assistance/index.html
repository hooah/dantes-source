What you need to know about financial assistance.

The U.S. Armed Forces offers several programs to support the education goals of service members (Army, Navy, Air Force, Marine Corps and Coast Guard) – including up to 100 percent financial assistance for college courses taken during off-duty hours.

 

Each service has its own criteria for eligibility, obligated service, application process and restrictions. This money is usually paid directly to the institution by the individual services. You are eligible as long as you remain active duty.

Army – 100 percent of tuition and fees, not to exceed:
  • $250 at semester credit hour
  • $166 at quarter credit hour
  • $4,500 at fiscal year
More Information

Marines – 100 percent of tuition and fees, not to exceed:
  • $250 at semester credit hour
  • $166 at quarter credit hour
  • $4,500 at fiscal year
More Information


Coast Guard – 100 percent of tuition and fees, not to exceed:
  • $250 at semester credit hour
  • $166 at quarter credit hour
  • $4,500 at fiscal year
More Information


Navy – 100 percent of tuition and fees, not to exceed:
  • $250 at semester credit hour
  • $166 at quarter credit hour
  • 16 semester hours at fiscal year
More Information


Air Force – 100 percent of tuition and fees, not to exceed:
  • $250 at semester credit hour
  • $166 at quarter credit hour
  • $4,500 at fiscal year
  • 75 percent of tuition and fees not to exceed:
    • $187.50 at semester hour
    • $3,500 at fiscal year
More Information

The Department of Veterans Affairs offers a variety of benefits and services that provide financial and other forms of assistance to service members, veterans, as well as their dependents and survivors.
