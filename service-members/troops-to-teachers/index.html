Troops to Teachers

  • Eligibility

    All current and former members of the U.S. Armed Forces whose last period of service is characterized as honorable may participate in the program for counseling and referral services. Those who meet certain education, military service and registration requirements may also be eligible to apply for TTT financial assistance to assist with their transition to the classroom.

  • Where will I teach?

    TTT provides teachers for grades pre-k through 12 at in-need public schools, charter schools and Bureau of Indian Affairs schools.

  • What will I teach?

    The program is geared toward placing teachers in such critical subjects as math, science, special education, foreign language and career-technical.

  • What does TTT provide?

    TTT provides counseling and referral services, and helps you identify certification requirements and programs leading to certification and employment opportunities.

  • How can I learn more about TTT?

    The TTT homepage is a portal that lists all state TTT offices that provide counseling and assistance regarding certification requirements, routes to state certification and employment leads. The TTT website also provides information and resource links, including links to state Departments of Education, state certification offices and other job listing sites in public education.

  • Troops to Teachers Research

    Owens Study

    Prior research on Troops to Teachers (TTT) from 2005 and 2009 using both principal surveys and measured student achievement affirms that Troops teachers are effective instructors, are likely to work in high-poverty, high-minority schools, teach critical subjects, use research-based instructional and classroom management practices, and plan to stay in the profession longer than traditionally prepared teachers with the same years of teaching experience. The Owens Study finds that the current TTT cohorts are meeting the same high benchmarks in these essential domains, identifies teacher preparation programs factors that helped them succeed in the classroom, and assesses these within the context of recent teacher effectiveness research.
