Troops to Teachers

Troops to Teachers (TTT) is a DANTES-managed Department of Defense (DoD) program that can help you begin a new career as a public school teacher â€“ giving you the opportunity to use your leadership skills, knowledge and experience to have a positive effect on our nation's youth.

For an overview of the TTT program, click here.

 


  • Eligibility

    All current and former members of the U.S. Armed Forces whose last period of service is characterized as honorable may participate in the program for counseling and referral services. Those who meet certain education, military service and registration requirements may also be eligible to apply for TTT financial assistance to assist with their transition to the classroom.


  • Getting Started

    Now that you have decided that you want to teach as a second career, how do you get there from here? If you have a couple of years to go before you will actually retire/separate, now is the time to get started. Many certification programs can take as much as two years to complete. Plan ahead and start now!

    The Getting Started PDF provides information you will need to become a teacher with TTT.


  • Registration

    The Step By Step PDF provides detailed steps to register for the TTT program.

    Returning User

    If you started but did not complete your online registration, click here to go to the TTT Online Registration Login page to complete and submit your registration. If you need assistance with or have forgotten your username or password, contact one of our counselors at ttt@navy.mil or phone 1-800-231-6242 ext. 3224 or 3157.


    First-Time User

    IMPORTANT: If you do not have an e-mail account, you cannot register online and will need to submit a paper registration. Click here to download the registration package containing instructions and registration form. The completed and signed registration can be submitted by:

    • Mail: DANTES, Troops to Teachers, 6490 Saufley Field Rd, Pensacola, FL 32509-5243
    • Fax: 850-452-1194
    • Scan/E-mail: TTTRecord@navy.mil

    Online registration for the program will include the following steps:

    1. Create a username and password.
    2. Login using your new username and password.

    3. Complete the online registration which will require the following information:

      • vocational interests, certification program and/or licensure information, if applicable
      • personal and contact information
      • military service information
      • teaching and educational background information
      • Pre-K through 12 referral preferences

      NOTE: Fields marked with an asterisk (*) are required to submit your application. Your information will be saved as you complete each step; however, your information will not be submitted to TTT for review until completion of Step 4.

    4. Review/validate the information you entered and authorize the release of personal information.

    NOTE: Your registration will not be submitted to TTT unless you accept the terms outlined in the "Registration Validation/Release of Information Agreement" section.

    If you have not previously registered with TTT, click here to register online.

  • Where will I teach?

    TTT provides teachers for grades pre-k through 12 at in-need public schools, charter schools and Bureau of Indian Affairs schools.

  • What will I teach?

    The program is geared toward placing teachers in such critical subjects as math, science, special education, foreign language and career-technical.

  • What does TTT provide?

    TTT provides counseling and referral services, and helps you identify certification requirements and programs leading to certification and employment opportunities.

  • How can I learn more about TTT?

    The TTT homepage, ProudToServeAgain.com, is a portal that lists all state TTT offices that provide counseling and assistance regarding certification requirements, routes to state certification and employment leads. The TTT website also provides information and resource links, including links to state Departments of Education, state certification offices and other job listing sites in public education.
